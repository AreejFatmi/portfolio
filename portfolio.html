ABOUT
PROBLEMS

People want to cook at home but find it challenging because making home cooked meals is time consuming which often results in dining out or ordering in.

SOLUTION

Introducing Oui, Chef!— Restaurant quality recipes, 5 ingredients, and under 45 minutes by world-renowned chefs. Oui, Chef! also encompasses voice recognition technology for chef led instructional videos. Users can play, pause, stop, forward and rewind recipe videos via voice commands. Goodbye to dirty screens and hello delicious home cooked food.

MY ROLE

This project is a collaboration between myself and fellow UX researchers, Anna Sybingco, Saru Arora and Yue Gao.

TOOLS

TIMELINES

01.
RESEARCH
RESEARCH PLAN

We conducted 5 interviews in order to discover what steps users follow when cooking at home. We discovered there was a common theme of cooking at home to be time consuming.

We surveyed 130 participants, ages 18-38, who on average cook 3-5 times a week.

SURVEY RESULTS

Time strained users between the ages of 18-38 enjoy cooking at home but have trouble finding recipes that are within a reasonable time frame.

67.5% refer YouTube to find recipes, 62.7% refer google

74.2% prefer video tutorials 81.3% choose a recipe based on what ingredients they have

AFFINITY DIAGRAM

We categorized our findings from our user research interviews in an affinity map and came up with a user persona.

USER PERSONA

After categorizing our findings in an affinity diagram, we came up with a user persona that represents our target user.

02.
IDEATE
FEATURE PRIORITIZATION MATRIX

As a group, we were brimming with ideas for our potential app. We wrote down all of our ideas on Miro, dot voted and organized them within a feature prioritization matrix.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

We conducted a competitor analysis on our top four competitors to understand the current market and develop a competitive edge over our competitors.

03.
PROTOTYPE
WIREFRAME SKETCHES

Everyone sketched their own versions of the proposed app.

LO-FI WIREFRAMES

We upgraded to lo-fi wireframes and chose the best features from each individual wireframe to combine our ideas into one mid-fi prototype.

GUERILLA TESTING

We conducted our first round of user testing with 5 participants, analyzed results and iterated. We conducted a second round of testing on the iterated prototype with another 5 participants for final iterations.

ITERATION

1. Tester: “Confused about the role of the icons on the recipe page.”

Before

When the user clicks on a recipe, they’re directed to the recipe page without icon guides.

After

Add screen guides for icons

2. Tester: “Saved - immediately booted out of the app, got kicked out of app because I didn’t sign up. It was very jarring for me”

Before: User would get booted out of the app when they wanted to save a recipe to their cookbook to sign up in order to use the feature.

After: We added a pop up informing the user to sign up for the app so they can save their favourite recipes.

3. Tester: “Confirmation that the user saved the recipe”

3. Tester: “Confirmation that the user saved the recipe” Before: When saving a recipe, the user didn’t receive a confirmation that their recipe was saved successfully.

After: We added a pop up to inform the user that their recipe was successfully saved.
